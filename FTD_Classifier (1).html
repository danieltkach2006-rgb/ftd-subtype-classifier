Genetic Testing Results
TARDBP encodes the TDP-43 protein. Mutations in this gene are associated with TDP-43 proteinopathy and frequently co-occur with ALS phenotypes.
MAPT encodes the microtubule-associated protein Tau. Mutations are strongly associated with Tau-type FTD (including Pick's disease) and typically present with behavioral-variant symptoms.
GRN mutations cause progranulin haploinsufficiency and are associated with TDP-43 proteinopathy. Patients often present with language-variant or asymmetric cortical features.
C9orf72 hexanucleotide repeat expansion is the most common genetic cause of both familial FTD and familial ALS. It is associated with TDP-43 pathology and may present with psychiatric symptoms.
FUS gene mutations are rare and associated with FUS-type FTD. These cases typically present at a notably younger age (often under 45) and may have rapid disease progression.
